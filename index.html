Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions about therapy, evaluations, and insurance.

Where is your office located?
The office is located at 1955 Merrick Road, Suite 205A, Merrick, New York 11566.
What if my child is hesitant to start therapy or come to the office?
We work hard to create a warm, comfortable, accepting environment for all ages. Building rapport is essential for success. Children can be slow to warm and our team has experience supporting kids in many settings, including schools and camps. We may incorporate games, prizes, and other kid friendly modalities to soften the experience while making the process rewarding.
Do you accept insurance?
At this time, we are not members of insurance panels. After payment, we provide a superbill you can submit to your insurance for potential out of network reimbursement. Please confirm your plan‚Äôs reimbursement and deductible details before starting.
How long is a typical therapy session or evaluation session?
Therapy sessions are typically 45 minutes. Evaluation length varies depending on testing performed and often takes 2‚Äì3 visits.
What is the ADOS-2?
ADOS-2 stands for Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule, 2nd Edition. It is a structured evaluation tool used alongside other measures to inform ASD diagnosis. ADOS-2 is rarely used in isolation and is often part of a more comprehensive evaluation.
How long will it take to feel better?
Everyone is different. Progress can ebb and flow. Some people experience quick results while others benefit from a longer term trajectory. The consistent foundation is a strong, trusting relationship with your clinician.
What is your rate for services?
We will inform you by phone and/or email of the specific session or evaluation fee based on your needs.
How long will evaluations take?
Evaluations often take around 4 weeks from start to finish, including information gathering, testing, report writing, and the feedback session (all included in the evaluation fee).
Good Faith Estimate (No Surprises Act)

You have the right to receive a Good Faith Estimate explaining how much your medical care will cost. If you receive a bill that is at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate, you can dispute the bill. For more information, visit cms.gov/nosurprises.
